From Ethiopian Highlands to Global Delight: A Coffee Odyssey

Coffee's origin lies in Ethiopia's highlands. According to legend, 9th-century goatherd Kaldi discovered coffee's stimulating properties when his goats became lively after nibbling on the berries. The beans' journey continued through the Arab world, flourishing in coffee cultivation. By the 17th century, coffee reached Europe, igniting a coffeehouse culture that fueled intellectual discussions and creativity. Today, coffee plantations worldwide, from Colombia's misty mountains to Vietnam's vibrant hills, impart unique flavors to the cherished bean.
